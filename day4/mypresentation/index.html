Definition of a data policy for marine research data in the Institute of Marine Sciences

Created by Alessandro Sarretta / @alesarrett
alessandro.sarretta@ismar.cnr.it

Questions to answer in presentations

  • What are the objectives of your project?
  • What do you expect to achieve in the next year?
  • What activities are you planning to do to meet those objectives?
  • What are the greatest challenges you expect to face?
  • What partners are you planning to involve?
  • How will you measure your success?

What are the objectives of your project?

build a collaborative (marine) research environment where sharing is the core

What do you expect to achieve in the next year?

  • focus on RITMARE national project
    • Institutional level later with good examples and practices
  • start a process to increase the number of data that are shared in the marine community
  • showcase concrete actions

What activities are you planning to do to meet those objectives?

  • reviewing of existing RITMARE data policy
    • translate existing RITMARE data policy
    • share it with IOL peers and ask specific feedback on how to improve it
    • start a review process in the RITMARE project
  • work on the "RITMARE Open research data publishing funds pilot
    • prepare a call for proposals (based on best practices)
    • promote it: factsheets, events
    • (activation of peer evaluation)
    • help desk for questions and clarifications
    • assessment of progress and outputs and proposal of follow-up, improvement (financial constraint)
    • template, guidance, best practice for funding such a time of initiatives (partnering FP7 post-grant funding pilot?)
    • final celebration

What are the greatest challenges you expect to face?

  • funds are not enough to attract interest from researchers
  • financial and temporal constraints to spread the word to everyone
  • scepticism and barriers (long time win perspective)

What partners are you planning to involve?

  • Director of Institute and RITMARE project
  • OPERAS (OPEn Research And Science group)
  • AISA - Association for dissemination of Open Science
  • IOL :-)

How will you measure your success?

  • feedback on existing data policy
  • number of applications received
  • number and quality of data papers published
  • personal interactions and feedback of the process
