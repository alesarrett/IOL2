#IOL2

Definition of a data policy for marine research data in the Institute of Marine Sciences

Created by Alessandro Sarretta / @alesarrett
alessandro.sarretta@ismar.cnr.it

Where I am in Venice

Context of my project

General problem in research and academics about sharing data

science should stand on the shoulders of giant and build on the best and timely existing knowledge to advance

but sometimes data are lying only in the desks of the researchers.

Major fears

  • getting scooped
  • loosing control of data
  • others can find errors (not so explicit but present)
  • "Others can misunderstand the data and draw wrong conclusions"

Other concerns

  • data are "my data" (even if paid by taxes)
  • I'm the only one able to understand
  • I don't have the time
  • I don't have the money
  • there are technical issues and skills to be considered when sharing (correctly) data

Research evaluation

Researchers and institutions are evaluated based on the IF of Journals they're publishing in, not the level of sharing or collaboration

Initiatives at European level are driving the process (e.g. H2020), not really at national level

Objective of my project

  • Make data become visible and usable by colleagues
  • Create a research data policy for ...?
    • Institute
    • National marine community
    • Euopean-level projects/network

Need a flexible policy

    Allowing different steps of application and different levels of sharing and openness, different licences and rules from different types of data?


  • Data are different per:
    • spatial scale, temporal dimension, quantity, standards, quality check procedures (biological samples on coasts, surveys from oceanographic vessels (local seas, ocean, Antarctica), output from oceanographic modelling dealing with climate change, data on genetics)

Where we are now

Data policy for RITMARE (www.ritmare.it)
  • embargo period changing depending on type of data
  • 2 years research collaboration period
  • after that, CC Licence (suggested)

FP7 post-grant Open Access publishing funds pilot

https://postgrantoapilot.openaire.eu
  • Additional instrument to make FP7 project research results Open Access
  • Will cover OA Article Processing Charges (APCs) for FP7 projects up to two years after they end.
  • A maximum of three publications per FP7 project will be funded.
  • Funded publications must be peer-reviewed and be made available under a CC-BY licence where possible
  • Publications should be deposited into an OpenAIRE-compliant repository

Statistics

https://postgrantoapilot.openaire.eu/#statistics

  • 274 publications from 235 projects have been approved for funding since 1/1/2015
  • A total of 282,460 EUR has been paid for APCs, with an average of 1,519 EUR per publication

RITMARE Open research data publishing funds pilot

  • Trying to replicate the FP7 Post-grant OA fund in our project
  • It's a small (20ĸ€) fund to publish data papers in open data journal, so that to have the data published in CC-BY

My questions

  • Where to start from?
  • Do we need a flexible and modular policy or a comprehensive one?
  • CC-BY or CC0 for research data?
    • very sensible issues... not yet touched
  • What about institutional research data repositories?
